Roast Beef Tenderloin with Mushroom Ragout

Ingredients Quantity
Beef tenderloin roast (center-cut) 2 pounds
Kosher salt and black pepper To taste
Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
Unsalted butter 3-5 tbsp
Mixed mushrooms (shiitake, cremini, white button) 1 pound
Shallot (chopped) or small onion 1 medium or ½ small
Fresh thyme (leaves stripped) 3 sprigs
Madeira, vermouth, or white wine ½ cup
Heavy cream ⅓ cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Heat a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Pat the beef dry and season with salt and a generous amount of pepper.
  3. Add the oil to the skillet and heat until shimmering. Add 1 tbsp butter and swirl to melt.
  4. Add the beef and sear until mahogany brown on all sides (about 8 minutes). Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast until an internal temperature of 125°F is reached for medium-rare (about 25 minutes).
  5. For the ragout: Heat 2 tbsp butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and spread evenly; increase heat to high.
  6. Let the mushrooms cook undisturbed until browned, then shake the skillet to flip. If the skillet becomes dry, add more butter along the edges.
  7. Cook mushrooms until nicely browned (about 5 minutes). Add shallots and cook until softened (about 2 minutes). Season with ½ tsp salt, pepper, and thyme.
  8. Remove from heat and add the Madeira. Return skillet to heat and scrape up brown bits. Add heavy cream and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and keep warm.
  9. Once beef is done, transfer to a cutting board, loosely tent with foil, and let rest for 10-15 minutes.
  10. Slice the beef across the grain, arrange on a platter, and serve with the mushroom ragout.
